Libby UX Improving the book exploration experience

Timeframe
April 2023, 1 month
Team
Solo
Fields
UX, UI, Motion Graphics
Tools
Figma, Illustrator, After Effects

Project Goal

Improve the book discovery process of digital library app Libby–test for obstacles, then design solutions.

Libby is a portal to the local library’s digital catalog. The redesign will start with research and deliver a redesigned experience as necessary.

Book discovery process = the experience of finding a title you’d be interested in borrowing.

Research + Findings

First, I had to learn about the users: their motivations & the general context.
Then, I went problem hunting by conducting usability tests and UX analyses (with heuristics & accessibility guidelines).

Survey (5 respondents)

Respondents liked different genres,
but used the same data to decide what to read

(with this key descriptive data missing from the app).
  • Book preferences varied: Some liked a range of genres, others liked just one. Some knew what they liked and stuck to it, others were overwhelmed by the choices.
  • Descriptive info lacking: Information that respondents rated important for decision-making were lacking from the app’s title listings (such as user ratings). Users depend on snippets of information provided in title listings to decide what to borrow and peruse.
Usability Testing (10 participants)

Found there are 3 main userflows

  • Picky Reader (Only these participants struggled)
  • One-Mission
  • Recommendation Seeker
Also, minor confusion from icons & site structure.

The usability tests found that there are 3 main userflows (in regards to book exploration), with only picky readers experiencing frustrations:

  • Picky Reader: these participants know what types of books & combinations of genres they like. However, app does not have robust capabilities for detailed searching and filtering, leading to frustration.
  • One-Mission: these participants had a specific book in mind and simply used the search bar.
  • Recommendation Seeker: these depended on recommendations.

Testing also showed there were points of confusion:

  • Icon and structure confusion caused by Libby-unique icons that don’t have a descriptive text label, and site structure that isn’t clear at a glance. Both of these issues were observed in the usability testing as well.
Accessibility Analysis, Heuristics Analysis

Users slowed by information overload

and resurfacing of icons & site structure confusion.

Heuristics like Web Accessibility Guidelines and Hick’s Law were used to evaluate the current design, with standout issues being:

  • Users are slowed down by information overload, partially caused by redundant listings.
  • Icon and structure confusion validated by, and same as found in usability testing.

Design Goals

With the problems pinpointed, it was time to formulate goals for the redesign.

How might we help Picky Readers find titles that fit their specific tastes... while continuing to serve the other two userflows and addressing the discovered user needs and quality of life issues.

This was both an obstacle (for Picky Readers) and an opportunity (for the other two user flows).

User needs and quality of life issues include: providing user desired decision-making information, resolving icon and structure confusion, and decreasing information overload.

Ideation + Process

With a goal in mind, it was time to find solutions. I brainstormed features and iterated on the UI to solve UX issues, and made static and animated mock-ups.

Brainstormed Features

I brainstormed potential features, took inspiration from existing design solutions, and listed the strengths and weakness of each potential fix. Finally, I picked the features that would best address the obstacles and opportunities discovered in the research phase.

Iterated UI Design

Next, I set out to implement the features in a UI design. In general, my process was to sketch wireframes by hand to work out the general layout, then create a grayscale mock-up to work out the details, and then to finalize with colors and images. Each stage was an exploration, and sometimes even needed a step back to the previous stage.

Made Motion Study

I also made some motion studies to show the transition animations. I planned out the animation changes on paper, then animated using Adobe After Effects.

Design Solutions

The redesign focuses on addressing the one user flow with frustrations (Picky Readers), maintaining key features for the other two types of uses. The redesign also has other changes based on accessibility and heuristics analysis.

Personalized suggestions

Suggests titles based on user history (left). Users can also fine tune suggestions (right).

Provides a point of entry and reduces feeling of overwhelmingness. Comfortable and familiar to users as the feature is common on social media apps.

Great for both picky readers and recommendation seekers.

Public user curated collections

Allows users to group titles that fit their specific preferences (for instance, a category more specific than a genre) and share these with other users.

This serves both Recommendation Seekers as well as Picky Readers (as they can share and find titles that fit their taste but not conventional genres).

Fine-grain filters

Allows users to apply a detailed combination of filters. A search feature helps users find the fThe ABCs of Behavior Analysisilters they want. Perfect for Picky Readers.

Addressing the other userflows with Quick search, Librarian-curated lists

  • To help One-Mission users who go straight for the search bar, the redesign adds quick search to the main page.
  • To help Recommendation Seekers, the redesign keeps librarian-curated lists prominent.

Other key changes:

  • Efficient searching: Show key data such as genres in list view. This is key decision-making data (as found in user survey).
  • Clarified structure: Updated layout clearly communicates structure, that multiple lists are included.
  • Clarified icons using descriptive text: Replaced platform-specific icons with descriptive text.
  • Choice overload:
    • Collapsed lists into 1 clickable item (instead of listing out list items)
    • Combined format-redundant listings. Added descriptive tags and a format toggle instead.

Reflection

This project highlighted to me the importance of testing with real users. The obstacles and opportunities I discovered during research and analysis were very different from what I had personally expected. Testing ensured that I was addressing real issues.

If I had more time, I would like to create a prototype and conduct another round of usability testing. I would also have liked to design with a higher level of integrated animations in mind, as I only had transition animations in mind while designing.
